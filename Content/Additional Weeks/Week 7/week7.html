Order of Magnitude

Definition

The **Order of Magnitude** of a quantity is the nearest power of 10 to that value.

x≈10n

We ignore the "mantissa" (the leading digits) and focus purely on the scale ($n$).

Rounding Logic

How do we round? We look at the midpoint on a logarithmic scale: $\sqrt{10} \approx 3.16$.

  • If m<3.16: Round down ($10^n$)
  • If m>3.16: Round up ($10^{n+1}$)

Example: $4 \times 10^3 \rightarrow 10^4$

The Philosophy of the Napkin

  • Speed of Insight: Solve complex problems in seconds to see if an idea is worth pursuing.
  • Reality Checks: If your calculator says a person weighs $10^5$ kg, your OoM intuition flags the error instantly.
  • Navigating Uncertainty: In space, we often don't know the exact value. "Within a factor of 10" is often a triumph.

"It is better to be roughly right than precisely wrong." — John Maynard Keynes

The Fermi Problem

Enrico Fermi

The "Architect of the Nuclear Age" famously estimated the power of the Trinity atomic test by dropping scraps of paper into the shockwave.

He calculated the energy as roughly 10 kilotons before any instruments were read. The actual value was 18 kilotons.

The Goal: Getting within 1/2 an order of magnitude of the truth.

Stars in the Milky Way

  2. Percent of mass as Stars $\approx 10\%$
  3. Average star mass $\approx 1 M_{\odot}$

Case Study: Interstellar Flight

Travel Time to Proxima Centauri

Voyager 1 Velocity: $v \approx 10^4$ m/s

Distance to Proxima Centauri: $d \approx 10^{16}$ m

t=dv=1016104=1012 seconds

Result: $\approx 30,000$ years

(Using $1 \text{ year} \approx 3 \times 10^7 \text{ sec}$)

Physics Sprinkle: Atomic Scales

Problem: Atoms in a Star

The Sun has a mass of $M \approx 2 \times 10^{30}$ kg. It is mostly Hydrogen ($m_p \approx 2 \times 10^{-27}$ kg).

Estimate the number of atoms in the Sun.

The Solution Path:

N=103010-27=1057

Note: Using OoM, $2 \rightarrow 10^0$ and $1.67 \rightarrow 10^0$ because they are $<3.16$. Thus we stay at $10^{57}$.
