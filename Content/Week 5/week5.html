Primordial Beacons Discovered

The Euclid Space Telescope has successfully identified 31 ancient quasars, dating back to when the universe was only 5% of its current age (~670 million years after the Big Bang).

  • Mass Mystery: Black holes with hundreds of millions of solar masses existed far earlier than standard models predicted.
  • Cosmic Map: This find doubles the known count of high-redshift quasars, refining our view of the Epoch of Reionization.

The Origin

Ex Nihilo: Extrapolating to the Singularity

The Age of the Universe

Modern observations, including the latest data from Planck and JWST, confirm a beginning roughly 13.8 billion years ago.

T≈1H0

Simple Hubble Time Relation

The universe started in an extremely hot, dense state. As space expanded, the energy density dropped, allowing particles to condense from photons.

Spacetime Curvature

Critical Density

The universe's fate and shape are determined by the density/curvature parameter \(\Omega_0\).

  • Flat: Light travels in straight lines; expansion continues forever (at a slowing then accelerating rate).
  • Closed: Sphere-like; light paths converge; possible "Big Crunch".
  • Open: Saddle-like; light paths diverge; infinite expansion.

OBSERVATIONAL VERDICT

The Flatness Result

Current CMB measurements from Planck indicate that we live in a Flat Universe (\(\Omega \approx 1.0\)) within a margin of 0.4% error.

This requires the total energy density to be exactly at the "Critical Density".

Image Credit (Left): University of Oregon.

The Cosmological Principle

The foundational axiom of modern astrophysics: On a sufficiently large scale (\(>100 \text{ Mpc}\)), the Universe is both:.

Homogeneity

NO SPECIAL PLACES

The Universe has a constant average density. If you sample a large enough volume of space anywhere in the cosmos, it will contain the same amount of matter and energy as any other sample.

Isotropy

NO SPECIAL DIRECTIONS

The Universe looks the same in all directions to any observer. There are no preferred axes of expansion or structural alignment on the largest scales.

The Copernican Extension

"We do not occupy a privileged or central position in the Cosmos."

This principle enables the FLRW Metric, allowing us to solve Einstein's field equations for the entire Universe at once.

The Cosmic Microwave Background

The First Light

Detected at 2.73 K, the CMB is the fossil radiation from the moment the universe became transparent (Recombination) roughly 380,000 years after the Big Bang.

  • Isotropy: The radiation is nearly identical in every direction.
  • Anisotropy: Tiny fluctuations (\(\mu K\)) are the gravitational seeds of every galaxy we see today.

Universal Composition

  • Dark Energy (69%)
  • Dark Matter (26%)
  • Ordinary Matter (5%)

"Ordinary matter—the stuff of stars, planets, and people—is a mere dusting on a cosmic landscape dominated by invisible forces."

Cosmic Inflation: The Grand Stretch

TIME: \(10^{-36}\) SECONDS

Exponential Expansion

In a fraction of a second, the universe expanded by a factor of at least \(10^{26}\), growing from smaller than an atom to roughly the size of a grapefruit.

  • Horizon Problem: Explains why widely separated regions have the same temperature.
  • Flatness Problem: Inflation stretched the curvature of space until it became effectively flat.

Quantum Entanglement & Cosmic Seeds

Early Entanglement

Before inflation, the entire observable universe was small enough to be quantum-mechanically linked. Particles were entangled, meaning their states were instantaneously correlated regardless of distance.

Image Credit: Nature Photonics, Zia et al.

Quantum to Classical

As inflation occurred, these subatomic quantum fluctuations were "frozen" and stretched into macroscopic scales. This process turned quantum probability blips into the density variations seen in the CMB.

ΔT/T ≈ 10⁻⁵

The magnitude of fluctuations that seeded all galaxies.

Baryogenesis: The Survival of Matter

1:10⁹
Matter-Antimatter Ratio

A Cosmic Asymmetry

The Big Bang should have produced equal amounts of matter and antimatter, which would have annihilated into pure light. However, a slight preference for matter—Baryogenesis—occurred.

For every billion particles of antimatter, there were a billion and one particles of matter. We are the leftovers of that single extra particle.

The Holographic Principle

Information at the Boundary

Emerging from black hole thermodynamics and the early Big Bang, this principle suggests that the total "information" of the 3D universe can be represented on a 2D boundary.

  • Entanglement Entropy: Modern cosmology explores if space itself is "woven" together by quantum entanglement.
  • Bit Density: The maximum information content depends on the surface area, not the volume.

Physics Sprinkle: Calculating the Age

Given the Hubble Constant $H_0 \approx 70 \text{ km/s/Mpc}$, estimate the age of a universe without dark energy dominance.

T=1H0

Step-by-Step Logic

  • Convert Units: Change Mpc to km to cancel the length unit.
  • Reciprocate: The result is in seconds. Convert to billions of years.
  • Result: Approx 14 billion years. (Remarkably close to the calibrated 13.8B!)
