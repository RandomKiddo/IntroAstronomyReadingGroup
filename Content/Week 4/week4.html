Recap: Galactic Morphology

Classification Schema

Edwin Hubble organized galaxies based on their visual appearance into a "tuning fork" diagram:

  • Ellipticals (E): Spherical to oval, containing mostly old stars and little gas.
  • Spirals (S/SB): Disk-shaped with spiral arms and active star formation.
  • Irregulars: Lacking a defined shape, often due to mergers.

Recap: Rotation Curves

The Missing Mass

Observations of spiral galaxies show that stars in the outer disk move faster than predicted by visible matter alone.

The Paradox:

Flat rotation curves imply that mass does not decrease as we move away from the center.

This provides critical evidence for a Dark Matter Halo extending far beyond the visible disk.

Recap: Distance Ladder

Standard Candles

Objects of known intrinsic luminosity used to measure distance. Includes Cepheid Variables and Type Ia Supernovae.

Distance Modulus

m-M=5log10(d)-5

Relating apparent (m) and absolute (M) magnitude to distance (d).

The Expansion Law

v = H₀d
The Hubble-Lemaître Law

The discovery that a galaxy's recessional velocity is proportional to its distance from us. This proves that the Universe is expanding uniformly at every point.

Space itself is stretching, carrying galaxies away from one another like raisins in a rising loaf of bread.

The Cosmic Web

Large Scale Structure

Matter is not distributed evenly. It forms a network of filaments and sheets, with vast empty voids in between.

Galaxies and clusters reside at the intersections of these filaments, driven by the gravitational collapse of dark matter.

Physics Sprinkle: Data Probe

01. Recession Speed

Assume the Hubble Constant is:

H₀ = 70 km/s/Mpc

If a galaxy cluster is 150 Mpc away, calculate its recessional velocity in km/s.

02. Redshift Logic

A galaxy displays a significant redshift in its spectrum. Explain why this shift is considered Cosmological rather than a standard Doppler effect.

Consider the role of expanding space vs. individual motion through space.

Solution: Recession Speed

Working the Math

We use the linear relation established by Hubble:

v=H0⋅d
  • Substitute: \(v = 70 \times 150\)
  • Result: \(v = 10,500 \text{ km/s}\)

Scale Context:

This velocity is roughly 3.5% of the speed of light. At this distance, the expansion of the universe is a dominant effect over any local "peculiar" motion of the galaxy within its cluster.

Solution: Redshift Nature

The "Fabric" Analogy

Unlike a Doppler shift (caused by an object moving *through* space), Cosmological Redshift is caused by the stretching of space itself.

As a photon travels across the expanding universe, its wavelength is stretched along with the fabric of spacetime, shifting it toward the red end of the spectrum.

  • Doppler: Change in wavelength due to relative velocity between source and observer.
  • Cosmological: Change in wavelength due to the increase in the scale factor of the universe during light travel.
