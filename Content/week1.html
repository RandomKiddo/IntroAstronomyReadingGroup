Astronomy in the News

JWST Revisits the Deep Field (2022)

By returning to iconic Hubble Deep Field regions and massive galaxy clusters, JWST's infrared eyes have fundamentally advanced our mapping of space-time curvature.

  • Hubble vs. JWST: Where Hubble saw empty patches or blurry smudges, JWST's resolution exposes thousands of ultra-distant galaxies and intricate, elongated cosmic arcs.
  • Strong Gravitational Lensing: The massive foreground galaxy clusters act as natural magnifying lenses, bending background light according to Einsteinian General Relativity.
  • Dark Matter Mapping: Analyzing the specific distortion patterns and image multiplications allows astrophysicists to build hyper-sharp maps of invisible dark matter scaffolding.

Note the warped, concentric orange arcs: these are distant background galaxies being lensed by the central cluster mass.

The Celestial Sphere

The Mapping System

Astronomers use a coordinate system projected onto an imaginary sphere around Earth.

  • Zenith: Point directly overhead.
  • Ecliptic: The Sun's annual path.
  • Equator: Projection of Earth's equator.

Antique Skies

A History of Mnemonics

Constellations were never scientific clusters; they are 2D projections of stars at vastly different distances. Historically, they served as navigational waypoints and seasonal calendars for agricultural societies.

Today, we use them as "International Postal Codes" for the 88 formal regions of the sky.

Seasonal Solar Variation

Why distance doesn't matter: Earth is closest to the Sun in January (Perihelion), but the Northern Hemisphere is tilted away.

Measuring with Geometry

Circa 240 BCE, using nothing but shadows in Alexandria and a well in Syene, the Earth's size was calculated with <2% error.

C = 360∘ θ × s

Ancient Ingenuity

The Experiment

Eratosthenes observed that at noon on the summer solstice, sunlight fell directly down a well in Syene, while it cast a shadow at an angle of 7.2° in Alexandria.

Recognizing that 7.2° is 1/50th of a circle, he concluded the distance between the cities was 1/50th of Earth's circumference.

Lunar Phases & Geometry

Synchronous Rotation

The Moon rotates at the same rate it revolves. We always see the same side, but the Sun illuminates different parts.
